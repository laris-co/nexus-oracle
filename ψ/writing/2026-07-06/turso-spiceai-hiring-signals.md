# Turso/ChiselStrike Inc. and Spice AI — Hiring Analysis (2025–2026)

## Turso (ChiselStrike Inc.)

**Current state of the formal careers page**: `turso.tech/careers` and every job sub-page under it (`/careers/senior-platform-engineer`, `/careers/database-internals-engineers`) now return HTTP 307/308 redirects to the homepage — the dedicated careers site has been quietly retired. The last time the Wayback Machine captured a live version of the page was **Feb 19, 2025**, and it listed exactly **one open role**: [Senior Platform Engineer](http://web.archive.org/web/20250209031927/https://turso.tech/careers/senior-platform-engineer) — Go-first (with Rust as a plus), AWS, Docker/Kubernetes, distributed systems, remote but restricted to North/Central/South American time zones. Turso had also promoted this same role on X/Twitter in Dec 2024 ([tweet](https://x.com/tursodatabase/status/1868703172052885597)). Third-party boards confirm the page is now empty: [Built In](https://builtin.com/company/turso/jobs) and [Built In Toronto](https://builtintoronto.com/company/turso/jobs) both show "No jobs to discover at this time."

**What's actually driving hiring instead — open-source-to-employee pipeline**: Turso's real 2025–2026 hiring channel is its "Faces of Turso" blog series, which repeatedly documents engineers being hired *directly out of* contributing to the company's from-scratch Rust rewrite of SQLite (originally codenamed "Project Limbo," officially renamed "Turso" in 2025) — [Meteorgan (Sept 2025)](https://turso.tech/blog/faces-of-turso-meteorgan), [Seon Woo Kim (Sept 2025)](https://turso.tech/blog/faces-of-turso-seon), [Mikaël Francoeur (Oct 2025)](https://turso.tech/blog/faces-of-turso-mikael), [Ihor (Nov 2025)](https://turso.tech/blog/faces-of-turso-ihor), and most strikingly a developer [hired while incarcerated](https://turso.tech/blog/working-on-databases-from-prison) after his Limbo contributions impressed the team. The rewrite itself is the recruiting funnel: the CEO's post [We will rewrite SQLite. And we are going all-in](https://turso.tech/blog/we-will-rewrite-sqlite-and-we-are-going-all-in) (Jan 21, 2025) explicitly reframes the whole company around this bet, and background coverage ([Changelog podcast #626](https://changelog.com/podcast/626), [HN discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=42378843)) notes contributor counts jumping from ~32 to 70+ once it became the flagship project — a much cheaper filter for systems talent than a job board.

**Scale and funding context**: Turso remains at seed stage — **$7M total raised** (2022 ChiselStrike seed, per [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/chiselstrike)) — with employee counts ranging **~19 (Tracxn)** to **~22 (PitchBook)** depending on source/date ([Tracxn](https://tracxn.com/d/companies/turso/__9yiGkZbmMa-YcuYPa2835J-NujMZua-HZOqt-ap7kg8), [PitchBook](https://pitchbook.com/profiles/company/491091-67)).

**What the mix reveals**: There is essentially zero visible hiring for sales, marketing, or support. Every signal — the one formal posting, the "Faces of" hires, the all-in blog post — points to a company pouring nearly all its (still-thin) headcount budget into database-internals/Rust systems engineering to finish rewriting SQLite, while deliberately not scaling a commercial go-to-market org yet. Recruiting itself has shifted from "post a job" to "watch who contributes well to the OSS rewrite and hire them" — a product-led, community-sourced hiring model rather than a conventional one.

## Synthesis / comparison

Both companies are small (~20-person), Rust-centric, "AI-native data infrastructure" startups with founders who came out of Big Tech systems teams (Turso: ex-ScyllaDB/Datastax; Spice AI: ex-Microsoft/Azure/GitHub), both deeply embedded in the Apache DataFusion/Arrow/Iceberg ecosystem, and both still at seed-stage funding levels. But their hiring postures diverge sharply:

- **Turso** has essentially stopped conventional recruiting — its own careers page redirects to the homepage — and instead converts open-source contributors to its from-scratch SQLite rewrite into hires. The technical direction (rewrite SQLite in Rust) *is* the hiring strategy. There is no visible GTM/sales hiring at all.
- **Spice AI** runs a standard ATS with a balanced, small slate of roles spanning core engineering (Rust/database engines), forward-deployed/customer engineering, first sales hire, and first DevRel hire — signaling a company consciously building out go-to-market motion in parallel with core product work, timed around its mid-2026 v2.0 release.
