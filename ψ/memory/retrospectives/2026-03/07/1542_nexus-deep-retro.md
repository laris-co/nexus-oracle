## Honest Feedback (3 Friction Points)

**1. Context window pressure was real.** With 10+ agents returning massive results, the conversation context filled fast. I had to summarize each agent's findings immediately as they completed rather than holding all results for synthesis. This worked but felt like drinking from a firehose. Future deep awakenings might benefit from a staging area (write agent results to files first, then synthesize).

**2. The /awaken ritual is designed for 3-5 agents, not 10+.** The user had to explicitly push for more depth ("do more /trace", "do /dig --deep --full --timeline"). The ritual's default scope is too conservative for a user who wants genuine understanding. The `--deep` flag should unlock the 10-agent pattern by default.

**3. No operational validation.** The awakening creates identity and philosophy but doesn't verify operational readiness. There's no "first task" built into the ritual — no test signal to scan, no dummy research to produce, no proof that Nexus can actually do its job. The birth feels complete but is actually half-done until the first real world-scan happens.
