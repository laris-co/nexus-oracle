# Learned: Spice.ai's video record reveals a GTM strategy the written record hides

- **Turso is entirely absent from Spice.ai's video content** — zero mentions across the official YouTube channel (56 videos, ~158 subscribers), conference talks (CMU DB seminar, AWS re:Invent 2025, Seattle DataFusion meetup), or podcast appearances — despite Turso being a real, load-bearing accelerator in the codebase (1,811 references, per [[2026-07-06_turso-vs-spiceai]]). Video/conference budget instead goes overwhelmingly to the AWS ecosystem (S3 Vectors, Bedrock, Titan, Nova). This is a real asymmetry between what the codebase supports and what the sales/marketing narrative promotes — the enterprise GTM is "centralized data lake acceleration for AWS," not "edge/embedded database acceleration," even though the latter is genuinely built and maintained.
- **DuckDB's narrative arc is visible on camera and shifts sharply**: celebrated as "the undisputed default local analytical engine" through 2024-2025 (CMU talk, early demos), then candidly critiqued in the Jan 2026 Cayenne-launch video for "memory bloat" and scaling failure on massive datasets — a public admission of the exact reliability tension independent benchmarks were also finding in this space. Cayenne (Vortex-based) is positioned as DuckDB's replacement for enterprise-scale workloads, while DuckDB stays in quickstart docs for ease-of-use. This is more candid than any blog post on the subject.
- **Company history confirmed independently via video**: Phillip LeBlanc's 2023 Protocol Labs "Founders" podcast appearance (243K views — by far Spice.ai's most-viewed video with a founder) is an unfiltered artifact of the original Web3/blockchain-data-indexing pitch (NFT fraud detection use case explicitly named) before the pivot — corroborates the GeekWire funding-announcement finding from earlier text research, now with primary-source video evidence.
- **Ballista/distributed-cluster adoption was a forced move, not proactive design**: the April 2026 Seattle DataFusion meetup talk (hosted at Databricks' own offices, ironically) reveals single-node vertical scaling was insufficient for their largest production customers — Ballista was adopted to fix a real limitation hit in production, not built speculatively ahead of need.
- **The Databricks-Neon acquisition is never discussed on camera anywhere** — even in the meetup literally hosted at Databricks' offices with Databricks' own DataFusion PMC chair (Andrew Lamb) present. Notable silence on a competitive event that Gemini's earlier market-ecosystem research flagged as the single biggest structural threat to Spice.ai's category.

Why this matters: video/talk content surfaces admissions and strategic emphasis that blog posts and docs smooth over — worth treating as a distinct research modality (not redundant with text research) whenever assessing a company's real priorities vs. its documented feature set.
